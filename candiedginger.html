Candied Ginger Slices

Gingerâ€“ an ingredient often praised for its health benefits makes its way into the candy-sphere with this candied ginger recipe. Yes, you could buy candied ginger at the store or in the bulk bins, but for me, homemade is always the way to go.

So when I thought of two ways to use a single ingredient I was pretty pumped. By boiling the ginger before candying it and making Ginger Teayou break down the fibers in the ginger ever-so-slightly to yield a softer product (also you get a delicious beverage out of it, so why not?). Just a note though, when candying the ginger, make sure not to stir the mixture otherwise the sugar will crystallize and turn into a big fat mess, trust me on that one.

Ingredients

  • 1 Cup of Boiled Ginger from the Ginger Tea recipe
  • 3 Cups of Reserved Water from the Ginger Tea recipe
  • 3 Cups of Granulated Sugar

Required Equipment

  • Large Pot
  • Wire Rack
  • Rimmed Baking Sheet
  • Silicone Mat or Parchment Paper

Directions

  1. Add reserved 1 cup of ginger slices from ginger tea recipe and 3 cups of water reserved from the ginger tea recipe to a large pot
  2. Pour in 3 cups of sugar and stir to combine
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil and let it boil for 45 minutes
  4. Remove the ginger slices and place them on the wire rack on top of the baking sheet linked with a silicone mat or parchment paper
  5. Let ginger cool for 30 minutes
  6. Toss in sugar & serve
