Chocolate Chip Banana Bread
Topped with Spiced Rum Caramel

So, I don't know about everyone else, but there's a few things I always have in my house– bananas, chocolate chips and an odd amount of spiced rum (from past holiday parties.) Bananas I usually seem to finish, but there's some weeks that I can't bring myself to peel one more.

When I feel like I can't ingest another banana I tend to turn towards the sweets, like this chocolate chip banana bread. Top it with caramel sauce, or not, but I promise you won't regret it. If you're not like me and never have spiced rum in your liquor cabinet, that's fine, you can substitute the spiced rum with any other dark liquor.

Chocolate Chip Banana Bread Topped with Spiced Rum Caramel

Ingredients: For the Bread

  • 2 Cups All Purpose Flour
  • 1 Tsp. Baking Soda
  • ½ Tsp. Baking Powder
  • 1 Cup Granulated Sugar
  • ½ Cup Vegetable Oil
  • 2 Eggs
  • 3 Mashed Bananas
  • 1 Tsp. of Vanilla Extract
  • 3 Tbsp. of milk (I used almond)
  • 1/2 Cup Semisweet Chocolate Chips

Ingredients: For the Caramel

  • 1 Cup Granulated Sugar
  • 1/4 Cup of Water
  • 1/2 Cup of Heavy Cream
  • 1 Tbsp. of Spiced Rum (or any dark liquor)
  • Sprinkle of Kosher Salt

Required Equipment

  • 2 Mixing Bowls
  • Measuring Cups & Spoons
  • Spatula
  • Loaf Pan
  • Saucepan
  • Stand Mixer

Directions: For the Bread

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 and grease your loaf pan with butter or baking spray
  2. Whisk together 2 cups flour, 1 teaspoon baking soda, ½ teaspoon baking powder in a mixing bowl & set aside
  3. In your stand mixer combine 1 cup granulated sugar and ½ cup vegetable oil with the paddle attachment
  4. Add eggs one at a time and mix on low to combine
  5. Slowly add the 3 mashed bananas (I added them one at a time) and vanilla and beat on low combine with mixture
  6. Add ⅓ of the dry mixture (make sure your mixer is on low) and combine, then add 1 tablespoon of milk, alternate this process until your dry mixture is fully combined
  7. Mix in the ½ cup of chocolate chips with your spatula
  8. Add the batter into the loaf pan
  9. Bake for around 60 minutes or until you insert a knife and it comes out clean

Directions: For the Caramel

  1. Simmer 1 cup or sugar and ¼ cup water on medium heat until it turns a deep amber color

  2. Remove the mixture from the heat & stir ½ cup of heavy cream and tablespoon of bourbon into the saucepan
  3. Add a pinch of kosher salt (optional), let cool & enjoy
