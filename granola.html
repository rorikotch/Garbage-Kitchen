Tropical Bulk Bin Granola

As a new zero-waster, I've been finding it difficult to find products I love in minimal or sustainable packaging, which to me, means I have to make it myself. So, I turned a basic granola recipe into a zero-waste recipe by using the bulk bins at my local Whole Foods.

Everything in the recipe was purchased in bulk, except for the candied orange peels which I made myself as well. By making my own granola I was able to save the waste by using mason jars at the bulk bins and I got all the fruits I liked in one recipe. Note: while making this recipe I was trying to use up my parchment paper instead of throwing it out, so use a silicone mat to make it all zero waste!

Ingredients

  • 3 Cups Old-Fashioned Oats
  • 1 Teapsoon of Kosher Salt
  • 1 Cup of Unsweetened Coconut Chips
  • 1/4 Cup of Flax Seeds
  • 1/2 Cup of Chia Seeds
  • 1/2 Cup of Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 1/2 Cup of Maple Syrup
  • 1/3 Cup of Slivered Almonds (*optional)
  • 1 Cup of Dried Mango
  • 1 Cup of Dried Pineapple
  • 1/4 Cup of Canded Manadrin Orange Peels

Required Equipment

  • Large Bowl
  • Measuring Cups & Spoons
  • Rimmed Cookie Sheet
  • Silicone Mat or Parchment Paper

Directions

  1. Preheat your own to 350 degrees and line a rimmed baking sheet with either parchment paper or a silicone mat.
  2. Combine 3 cups old-fashioned oats, 1 tsp. kosher salt, 1 cup unsweetened coconut chips, 1/2 cup maple syrup, 1/2 cup olive oil, 1/2 cup chia seeds, and 1/4 cup flax seeds in a large bowl
  3. Spread a layer of the mixture onto your rimmed baking sheet
  4. Cook for 12-15 minutes and toss the mixture halfway through the baking time
  5. Chop up all the dried fruit into roughly Â¼ inch pieces
  6. Let the granola mixture cool for 2-3 minutes
  7. Add the dried fruit pieces into the granola mixture & combine the mixture
