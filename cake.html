Melted Strawberry Ice Cream Cake
with Fresh Strawberry Syrup

After a long night of studying, I usually find myself indulging in some treats, one of them being ice cream. Recently, strawberry has been my favorite flavor, but I'm definitely guilty of leaving the ice cream on the counter by accident and dozing off.

Once the ice cream melts, I usually won't put it back into the freezer, but I figured, why not repurpose it into something else I like– cake. The ice cream in this recipe replaces all the oil and water that's called for on your typical boxed cake mix (this recipe would also work if you make your own cake mix). One can say this recipe isn't totally zero-waste, and to me, that's okay. It's about taking baby steps, right?

Ingredients

  • 1 box of cake mix of your choice
  • 1 Pint of Melted Ice Cream
  • 3 Eggs
  • 2 Cups of Confectioner's Sugar
  • 1 Teaspoon of Vanilla
  • 1/4 Cup of Milk (I used almond milk)

Required Equipment

  • Bundt Pan
  • Spatula
  • Knife & Cutting Board
  • Small Bowl

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees
  2. Cut strawberries into small pieces and set aside
  3. Pour cake mix into mixing bowl and crack 3 eggs into cake mix
  4. Pour in a pint of melted ice cream (make sure the ice cream is fully melted or the cake won’t bake through properly)
  5. Pour 1 cup of strawberries into the cake batter
  6. Mix & pour into a greased bundt pan
  7. Follow box’s baking instructions for the pan of your choice
  8. Allow time to cool on a wire rack or countertop
  9. Add the 2 cups of confectioners sugar, 1 tsp. of vanilla, ¼ cup of milk & remaining 1 cup of strawberries into a sauce pan
  10. Heat on low until combined & smooth
  11. Pour over cake & let soak in for around 30 minutes
