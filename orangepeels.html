Candied Mandarin Orange Peels

Ingredients

  • 4 Mandarin Orange Peels
  • 1 Cup Granulated Sugar
  • 3 & 3/4 Cups of Water

Required Equipment

  • 1 Large Pot
  • Cutting Board & Knife
  • Measuring cup
  • Silicone Mat or Parchment Paper
  • Oven-Safe Wire Rack
  • Cookie Sheet or Baking Sheet

Directions

  1. Wash your mandarin oranges with soap and hot water. Scour the skin of the fruit with a sponge or chainmail mesh to ensure the peel is clean.
  2. Dry the oranges with a towel and peel the oranges (keep the pith on.) Julienne the peel into thin strips
  3. Add the strips into a pot and add about 1 ¼ cups of water (or enough to cover the peels.)
  4. Simmer the peels for about 5 minutes and strain. Add the peels and fresh water back into the pot and repeat 1 more time.
  5. Strain the peels and add peels back to the pot with another 1 ¼ cups of water. Simmer for 30 minutes
  6. Strain the peel and reserve the water from the pot. Measure the water and add the same amount of sugar into the pot along with the peels and reserved water.
  7. Simmer the peels for around 30-45 minutes or until the peels become translucent and soft. (make sure not to stir the mixture otherwise the peels will crystallize.)
  8. Turn off the heat and allow the mixture to cool for around 5 minutes.
  9. Remove the peels with a fork and place the warm peels on a wire rack that is set on top of a cookie sheet.
  10. Place the cookie sheet with wire rack and peels into your oven at its lowest temperature for around 40 minutes or until the peels are dry.
  11. Immediately toss the peels in granulated sugar and store in an airtight container.
